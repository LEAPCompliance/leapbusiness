India's New Labour Codes, Explained

All 4 Labour Codes are now in effect nationwide, replacing 29 old central acts. Here's what changed, what's still rolling out state-by-state, and what it means for your business.

⚠️ This page is for general awareness and reflects our understanding of the Labour Codes as of July 2026. Rules are still evolving state-by-state. This is not legal advice — contact LEAP to confirm what applies to your business today.
The 4 Codes

One Framework, Replacing 29 Old Acts

The Codes consolidate decades of overlapping central labour legislation into four unified laws.

💰
Code on Wages, 2019

Consolidates the Minimum Wages Act, Payment of Wages Act, Payment of Bonus Act, and Equal Remuneration Act. Introduces a uniform "wages" definition and the 50% wage-floor rule.

🤝
Industrial Relations Code, 2020

Consolidates the Trade Unions Act, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, and Industrial Disputes Act. Covers standing orders, retrenchment, layoffs, and dispute resolution.

🛡️
Social Security Code, 2020

Consolidates EPF, ESIC, Gratuity, Maternity Benefit, and Employees' Compensation laws (9 acts total). Extends social security coverage to gig and platform workers.

🏭
OSH & Working Conditions Code, 2020

Consolidates the Factories Act, Contract Labour Act, Inter-State Migrant Workmen Act and 10 other acts covering workplace safety, health, and working conditions.

What's Happened So Far

Implementation Timeline

21 November 2025

All four Labour Codes notified as effective law nationwide. The 29 central labour acts they replace stand repealed.

8 May 2026

Central Government notifies final Central Rules for all four Codes — Code on Wages (Central) Rules 2026, Social Security (Central) Rules 2026, OSH&WC (Central) Rules 2026, and Industrial Relations (Central) Rules 2026.

Ongoing — as of July 2026

State-level rules are rolling out unevenly. Over 30 states/UTs have notified rules for at least one Code, but there is still no single common commencement date announced by the Centre for all state-dependent provisions.

Maharashtra Status

Where Maharashtra Stands

Maharashtra is among the states furthest along, having notified rules under most of the four Codes, alongside Gujarat and Karnataka. This means Maharashtra-based employers should expect state-level provisions to become fully operative sooner than employers in states that haven't yet notified their rules.

Definitions

Definitions: What's Changed

The Codes redefine several foundational terms — and those new definitions decide who counts as a worker, what counts as wages, and which rules apply where.

Worker

Excludes supervisors drawing ₹18,000/month or more (earlier the cut-off was just ₹3,000/month) — a big jump that pulls many mid-level supervisory staff out of "worker" protections.

Inter-State Migrant Worker

Now includes resident migrants working in the state as well as migrants brought in by a contractor, where wages are below ₹18,000/month.

Contract Worker

Now includes inter-state migrant workers and "out workers" — those who work outside the establishment's premises.

Establishment

A place of business, trade, or manufacture employing 10 or more workers.

Wages

Basic pay + DA + Retention Allowance. Excludes Bonus, HRA, other allowances, PF/Pension, overtime, and Gratuity. If these exclusions exceed 50% of total remuneration, the excess is added back to "wages" for benefits computation.

Appropriate Government

For contractors working in centrally-governed establishments, compliance now falls under the Central Labour Rules.

Registration & Licensing

Registration & Licensing: What's New

Registrations and contractor licences are being consolidated under the OSH Code — fewer overlapping registrations, but new deadlines to track.

1
Existing registrations under the Factories, BOCW, CLRA, ISMW, Mines, and Plantations Acts remain valid under the OSH Code, subject to intimation to the registration officer within the specified time. This also applies to EPF and ESIC registrations.
2
Registration under OSH must be obtained within 60 days of applicability. A single registration now covers all establishment details, contractors, contract workers, inter-state migrant workers, and construction work.
3
Closure of an establishment must be intimated within 30 days, along with a declaration certifying payment of all dues to workers. Surrender of any registration under the OSH Code must be accompanied by a similar declaration from the employer.
4
Contract licences are now required only for contractors employing 50 or more workers.
5
A contractor may obtain a single licence covering all its establishments in a state (and in some cases, nationally), with provisions to add new establishments to the licence based on fresh work orders.
Wages, Welfare & Benefits

Wages, Welfare & Benefits: What Changes for You

Work hours, leave, gratuity, and compensation rules have all shifted — most of them in the employee's favour, which means higher benefit costs for employers.

Work Hours & Overtime

8 hours a day, 6 days a week. Overtime is payable at twice the regular wage rate (as newly defined). Working on the weekly rest day is now also compensated at double rate.

Leave Entitlement

1 day of leave for every 20 days worked. Minimum 180 days worked in a year required (down from 240). Layoff, maternity, and annual leave count towards eligibility, though not towards entitlement itself. Holidays falling within a leave period don't count as leave.

Leave Carryover & Encashment

Maximum carryforward is 30 days; the excess can be encashed at year-end. Leave applied for but not granted can be encashed without limit. All leave entitlement is payable on discharge, dismissal, or superannuation, even if eligibility criteria aren't otherwise met.

New Wage Definition Impact

PF, ESI, Bonus, Maternity Benefit, and Gratuity are all now calculated on the new definition of wages — expect higher benefit costs across the board.

Gratuity

Fixed-term employees are now eligible for gratuity on a pro-rata basis after a minimum of 1 year's work. Payable within 30 days of the employee's application. Gratuity insurance is now mandatory.

Death / Disability Compensation

Calculated as 50% of wages multiplied by the relevant age factor. The earlier minimum compensation clauses of ₹1.2 lakh / ₹1.4 lakh under the Employee Compensation Act have been deleted.

Employer Obligations

Your Obligations as Employer

These are the operational obligations that fall directly on employers under the new Codes — the ones LEAP helps you stay on top of every month.

Payment of Wages

Wages must be paid within 7 days of the end of the pay period, for all establishments.

Limit on Deductions

Deductions from monthly salary cannot exceed 50% of wages, as newly defined.

Contract Labour Wages

The principal employer is directly accountable for the wages and benefits of all contract labour, and can deduct unpaid wages or contributions from contractor invoices.

Appointment & Discharge

A letter of appointment must be issued within 3 months of the Code becoming applicable. An experience certificate must be given to discharged employees within the stipulated days.

Safety Officers

Mandatory for factories employing 500 or more workers, hazardous industries with 250 or more workers, construction work with 250 or more workers, and mines employing 100 or more workers.

Full & Final Settlement

Must be completed within 2 days of relieving. Wages or benefits may not be reduced for any reason.

Annual Health Checkups

Must be provided free of cost to all employees, including contract employees.
