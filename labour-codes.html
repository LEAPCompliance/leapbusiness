India's New Labour Codes, Explained

All 4 Labour Codes are now in effect nationwide, replacing 29 old central acts. Here's what changed, what's still rolling out state-by-state, and what it means for your business.

⚠️ This page is for general awareness and reflects our understanding of the Labour Codes as of July 2026. Rules are still evolving state-by-state. This is not legal advice — contact LEAP to confirm what applies to your business today.
The 4 Codes

One Framework, Replacing 29 Old Acts

The Codes consolidate decades of overlapping central labour legislation into four unified laws.

💰
Code on Wages, 2019

Consolidates the Minimum Wages Act, Payment of Wages Act, Payment of Bonus Act, and Equal Remuneration Act. Introduces a uniform "wages" definition and the 50% wage-floor rule.

🤝
Industrial Relations Code, 2020

Consolidates the Trade Unions Act, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, and Industrial Disputes Act. Covers standing orders, retrenchment, layoffs, and dispute resolution.

🛡️
Social Security Code, 2020

Consolidates EPF, ESIC, Gratuity, Maternity Benefit, and Employees' Compensation laws (9 acts total). Extends social security coverage to gig and platform workers.

🏭
OSH & Working Conditions Code, 2020

Consolidates the Factories Act, Contract Labour Act, Inter-State Migrant Workmen Act and 10 other acts covering workplace safety, health, and working conditions.

What's Happened So Far

Implementation Timeline

21 November 2025

All four Labour Codes notified as effective law nationwide. The 29 central labour acts they replace stand repealed.

8 May 2026

Central Government notifies final Central Rules for all four Codes — Code on Wages (Central) Rules 2026, Social Security (Central) Rules 2026, OSH&WC (Central) Rules 2026, and Industrial Relations (Central) Rules 2026.

Ongoing — as of July 2026

State-level rules are rolling out unevenly. Over 30 states/UTs have notified rules for at least one Code, but there is still no single common commencement date announced by the Centre for all state-dependent provisions.

Maharashtra Status

Where Maharashtra Stands

Maharashtra is among the states furthest along, having notified rules under most of the four Codes, alongside Gujarat and Karnataka. This means Maharashtra-based employers should expect state-level provisions to become fully operative sooner than employers in states that haven't yet notified their rules.

Key Changes

What Employers Need to Know

New "Wages" Definition & the 50% Rule

Basic pay + DA must now equal at least 50% of total remuneration. If allowances push this below 50%, the excess is added back to "wages" for PF, gratuity and bonus calculation, raising employer statutory cost on allowance-heavy structures.

Gratuity for Fixed-Term Employees

Fixed-term employees become eligible for gratuity on a pro-rata basis without needing to complete 5 years of continuous service, unlike the earlier regime.

Social Security for Gig & Platform Workers

Gig workers, platform workers, and unorganised sector workers get a formal social security framework for the first time under the Code on Social Security.

Simplified Registration & Returns

Single registration and licensing regime and simplified return filing are intended to replace multiple overlapping registrations under the old acts.
